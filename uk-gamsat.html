How the UK route works

What UK applicants need to sort out first.

The common mistake is treating every UK school as if it uses GAMSAT the same way. It does not. Start with the schools where your score can realistically keep you in the running, then check the rest of the admissions process.

01

Choose schools early

ACER lists several UK programs that use GAMSAT, but eligibility and home or international status vary by school. Build your shortlist before the application rush.

02

Build the score first

Your GAMSAT result still decides whether some schools keep reading. This is where a tighter method helps more than another pile of random practice.

03

Submit UCAS on time

For the 2026 cycle, ACER lists 15 October 2026 as the UCAS deadline for 2027 entry.

04

Forward scores correctly

ACER says UK applicants must indicate UK selection information and complete score forwarding details by 31 October 2026.

FAQ

UK applicant questions

Can I join from the UK?

Yes. Everything is online, and we already work with students in the UK. Blueprint is the easiest option if you need maximum flexibility across time zones.

Do all UK schools use GAMSAT the same way?

No. ACER and school websites show different rules by university. Some rank heavily by GAMSAT before interview. Others combine degree class, GAMSAT, and extra selection criteria.

What is the most important thing to fix first?

Your score. A stronger GAMSAT result keeps more schools open and makes the rest of the application worth working on. That is why we start with the Score Leak Method, not generic motivation talk.

What if Section 3 is the only thing holding me back?

Go straight to private tutoring. If S3 is the bottleneck, focused help is often a better use of time than buying a wider course first.
