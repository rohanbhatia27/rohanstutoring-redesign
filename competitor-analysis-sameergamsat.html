4. Positioning & Messaging

Sameer — "Evidence-based, equitable, affordable"

  • Pedagogical authority: Bloom's taxonomy, named "skill clusters" (Spatial Geometry, Systems Equilibrium, Data Interpretation, Cognitive Loading). Reads academic and structured.
  • Equity angle: "You shouldn't have to break the bank to become a doctor." Affordability is the moral pitch.
  • Quant proof: 34% admit, 69 S3 median, +13 marks. Specific and compelling.
  • Comparison table baked into S1/S2 and S3 pages directly naming "GR" and "FG" (Gradready / Fraser's). Confident anchoring against the giants.
  • Tone: Earnest, scholarly, slightly clinical. Lots of curriculum detail.

Rohan — "Premium, founder-led, modern"

  • Emotional hook: "Stop guessing. Start getting in." A buyer's-pain headline rather than a method statement.
  • Anti-big-prep frame: Versus table is the centre of gravity. Same enemy as Sameer, sharper rhetoric.
  • Brand quality: Editorial/dark-luxe design, premium typography, motion. Reads as a brand, not a WordPress site.
  • Trust signals: Volume (1,300+), top 1%, named acceptances at every Australian med school logo wall, refund.
  • Tone: Confident, conversion-focused, marketing-led. Slightly less academic depth on the page itself.

5. SWOT — Rohan's GAMSAT (relative to Sameer)

Strengths
  • Brand & web experience. Custom static build, premium aesthetic, fast load. Sameer's site is functional WordPress with stocky AVIFs and inconsistent hierarchy.
  • Lead-magnet ecosystem. S2 Slam, S1 Mock, Quote Generator, Tracker, weekly webinar, study-plan quiz. He has practically none of this.
  • Tiering clarity. Three clean tiers (Blueprint / Comprehensive / Mastery) with pricing anchored against $5K incumbents. Sameer has 12+ SKUs across three product pages — harder to choose.
  • Risk reversal. Refund guarantee. He has none.
  • International reach. AU + UK + Ireland. He's AU-only.
  • Self-paced moat. Blueprint is a real digital asset that scales without your time.
  • Volume social proof. "1,300+ students" trumps his ~28 admissions/yr figure for trust at first glance.
Weaknesses
  • Pedagogical depth on-page. Sameer publishes a full 10-week curriculum, named skill clusters, and Bloom's framework. Your site says "frameworks" but doesn't show the system. Buyers comparing tabs will feel his depth.
  • Outcome stats are softer. "1,300+ coached" is volume. He shows admit rate, score median, score delta. Quant beats vibes for analytical buyers.
  • No MMI / interview offer. He captures the next funnel stage; you don't.
  • No S3-only entry SKU. Sameer's $100 weekly drop-in is a frictionless trial. Your cheapest live entry is $1,549.
  • Section 3 credibility on-page is thin compared to his very detailed S3 syllabus. Top-1% is broad; he names 92 raw in S3.
  • No public Google reviews badge / star rating visible on the homepage. He flexes 5.0 prominently.
Opportunities
  • Add an MMI product (or partner). He's proving the demand at $150–$600 price points; you already have the credibility and traffic.
  • Publish your method. Name the frameworks (you already say "section-specific methods" — name them, diagram them, show a 10-week curriculum). Match his pedagogy theatre.
  • Lead with a quant outcome stat alongside "1,300+". Even one number — "average +X marks", "Y% into med school" — closes the credibility gap.
  • Cheaper trial SKU. A $99–$199 single-week or single-section taster fights his entry tier without cannibalising Comprehensive.
  • UK/Ireland-specific landing page. He's not there. Owning "GAMSAT for UK/Ireland applicants" is defensible.
  • Add a visible Google reviews / Trustpilot badge on the homepage.
  • Founder-story content. His "Our Journey" timeline (2020 → 2025) gives him narrative depth. An equivalent story on your /about would humanise the brand.
Threats
  • Price wedge. A serious, price-sensitive S3-focused student can get 10 weeks of live S3 coaching from Sameer for $800. You don't have a like-for-like product under $1,549.
  • Pedagogical legitimacy. Bloom's-taxonomy framing is sticky with academic buyers and parents; if he scales SEO around it, he owns the "evidence-based" association.
  • SEO surface area. WordPress + active blog (free practice questions, low-GAMSAT options, etc.) is built for organic traffic. Static site limits how aggressively you can compete on long-tail content.
  • Direct attack on incumbents — his comparison table names Gradready and Fraser's by initials. If he pushes that further, the "us vs the giants" lane gets more crowded.
  • Personal-relationship risk. You appear on his testimonial wall. Aggressive head-to-head positioning could be reputationally awkward in a small AU med-tutoring market.
  • Founder-led capacity ceiling applies to both of you — but he's already systematised group classes (≤10) and a 3-person team. If he scales tutors faster, his unit economics improve before yours do.

7. Recommended Moves

  1. Name and visualise your method. He owns "Bloom's." You can own a different concept (e.g. a named 3-step "How We Teach" framework already on your homepage) — but name the steps, show the 10-week scope, and match his pedagogy theatre. Buyers comparing tabs need to feel equal depth.
  2. Add at least one quant outcome stat to the hero. "1,300+ students · average +X marks" or "Y% offer rate". This is the single biggest credibility gap on the page today.
  3. Build a lightweight MMI or interview offer. Even a 1-circuit mock at $200, 3-circuits at $499, mirrors his price ladder and plugs the post-GAMSAT funnel hole.
  4. Introduce a sub-$300 trial SKU. A single-week S3 drop-in or "Diagnostic + plan" intensive removes the price-wedge objection without hurting Comprehensive sales.
  5. UK/Ireland landing page. He's not there. Own "GAMSAT prep for UK and Ireland applicants" with country-specific copy and case studies.
  6. Surface the Google rating. If you have a 5★ rating or strong reviews, badge it on the homepage. He does, prominently.
  7. Strengthen S3 credibility. A specific raw-S3 number (yours or a top tutor's) closes the gap with his "92 raw S3" anchor.
  8. Hold the high road on him personally. Differentiate on system, brand, geography, risk reversal, self-paced asset — not on attacking him. The market is small and you have a personal history; the anti-big-prep framing already does the heavy lifting.

8. One-Line Summary

Sameer wins on academic depth, outcome statistics and price-per-section. You win on brand, self-paced asset, risk reversal, lead-magnet funnel and international reach. The fastest gap-closers on your side are: (1) publishing a named curriculum, (2) adding one hard outcome stat, and (3) launching even a minimal MMI product to plug the funnel.
