Fair advice. Incomplete advice.

Writing more only helps if each essay teaches you something specific before the next one.

Otherwise you can write twenty essays and just get very fluent at making the same mistake.

This is where Section 2 gets frustrating. Students often know their essays are not quite working, but they cannot see the exact reason. The argument feels thin. The examples feel forced. The conclusion sounds polished but does not really land.

So they try to fix everything at once.

More philosophy. More examples. More vocabulary. More structure.

Usually, the fix is narrower than that.

One paragraph is not earning its claim.

One example is doing too much.

One idea is being repeated in different clothing.

One sentence sounds impressive but avoids the prompt.
