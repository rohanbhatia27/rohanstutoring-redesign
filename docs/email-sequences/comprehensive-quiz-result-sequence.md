## Email 4: Essay feedback changes the work

Send: Day 5

Subject: "Lacks depth" is useless feedback

Preview text: Your essays do not need vague comments. They need someone to show you what changed the score.

CTA: See the Comprehensive Course

Body:

Hi {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Most essay feedback is too vague to be useful.

"Needs more depth."

"Be more nuanced."

"Improve structure."

Fine. But what do you do with that on Tuesday night when you are staring at a blank document and the clock is running?

Useful feedback is more specific.

This paragraph is making a claim it has not earned.

This example is doing too much work.

This sentence sounds clever but does not move the argument.

This is where the essay stops answering the prompt.

That kind of feedback changes the next essay. Which is the only kind that matters.
