# Design Context: Rohan's GAMSAT

## Users & Purpose

**Who they are:** Ambitious Australian GAMSAT sitters, primarily late teens to late 20s, with some older career-changers. Serious about medicine and seeking a competitive edge.

**Context:** Landing on the site after searching for premium GAMSAT prep. They're tired of mass-market coaching and want to feel they've found expert mentorship.

**Job to be done:** Get clear on whether this approach is sharper and more credible than big prep companies. Feel guided by someone who genuinely understands the exam at depth.

**Emotional goals:** Clarity, trust, confidence, and the sense that "someone at the top level is guiding me."

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Sharp, premium, mentor-like

**Voice:** Expert, perceptive, ahead of the curve. Approachable but authoritative. High-trust. Like a private mentor—refined and serious, but not distant or intimidating.

**What they should remember:** This brand offers a sharper, more thoughtful, deeper approach than big prep companies. Real insight, not recycled content.

**What this explicitly is NOT:** Sales-funnel marketing, templated coaching vibes, startup-SaaS energy, try-hard hype, or anything that looks AI-generated.

---

## Aesthetic Direction

**Overall feel:** Editorial, polished, intentional. Like a high-end education brand or financial advisory firm—tasteful and confident without coldness.

**Theme:** Light base with strong dark accents (refined cream/off-white paired with deep navy or charcoal). Not full-dark, not pastel-light.

**Color approach:** Sophisticated and distinctive. If blue remains, it should be deep and rich, not bright or generic tech-blue. Avoid anything loud, childish, or obviously templated.

**What premium means here:** Refined, intelligent, high-trust. Elevated and selective, but still approachable. The design should whisper "I understand" rather than shout.

**Visual principles:**
- Typography and spacing matter more than effects or color
- Restraint over excess; hierarchy over clutter
- Avoid over-animation, glassmorphism, and obvious design moves
- Bespoke and intentional, never templated or lazy
- Credibility and depth communicated through thoughtful choices, not decoration

---

## Design Principles

These principles guide all design decisions going forward:

1. **Clarity first.** Every element should earn its place. Avoid visual noise and hierarchical confusion.

2. **Restraint.** Premium doesn't mean maximum; it means choosing exactly what's needed and executing perfectly. Whitespace is a tool.

3. **Mentor-like tone in design.** Approachable but authoritative. The visual language should feel like guidance from someone who's been there, not a sales pitch.

4. **Typography as the hero.** Refined type choices, thoughtful hierarchy, and generous spacing communicate intelligence and taste. This is where premium lives, not in color or effects.

5. **Editorial polish.** Think luxury brands, quality education, financial advisory. Polished, intentional, and timeless—not trendy or gimmicky.

6. **Trust through credibility.** Design choices should reinforce expertise and depth. Avoid the visual tells of generic AI output; every decision should feel deliberate.

7. **Light elegance over dark drama.** A refined light base with purposeful dark accents. Elegant and sophisticated, not theatrical.
